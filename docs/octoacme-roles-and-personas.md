# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers focus on user-centered design and usability throughout the project. They create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications to ensure solutions are intuitive and meet user needs.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and gather requirements from end-users
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Advocate for usability and accessibility in all project decisions
- Collaborate with Product Managers to translate customer needs into design
- Participate in design reviews and provide feedback on implementation
- Define and maintain design systems and UI patterns
- Support QA in acceptance testing of user-facing features

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and delightful user experiences
- Reduce user friction and support adoption
- Ensure design consistency across the product

### Typical Communication
- Design kickoff meetings with Product and Engineering
- Weekly design sync with stakeholders
- Design critique sessions and code review comments
- Usability testing reports and insights

### Collaboration Touchpoints
- **Product Manager**: Align on user research findings and feature prioritization
- **Developers**: Review implementation against design specifications; provide guidance on UI/UX edge cases
- **QA/Testing**: Collaborate on user acceptance criteria and usability testing plans
- **Project Manager**: Support timeline planning for design activities and design review cycles

---

## Tech Lead

### Role Summary
Tech Leads provide technical leadership and architectural guidance. They oversee design decisions, resolve technical challenges, and ensure solutions are scalable, maintainable, and aligned with engineering best practices.

### Responsibilities
- Own technical architecture and design decisions
- Oversee code quality and technical standards across the team
- Identify and resolve key implementation risks and challenges
- Mentor developers and conduct technical code reviews
- Estimate technical complexity and propose solutions
- Collaborate on trade-offs between features, performance, and maintainability
- Lead design review sessions for complex components

### Goals
- Deliver scalable, maintainable, and performant solutions
- Build team capability and reduce technical debt
- Enable efficient decision-making on technical trade-offs

### Typical Communication
- Technical design documents and architecture reviews
- Code review comments and technical guidance
- Weekly sync with Project/Product leadership on feasibility
- Technical deep-dive sessions with the development team

### Collaboration Touchpoints
- **Developers**: Provide architectural guidance, code reviews, and mentorship
- **Project Manager**: Assess technical feasibility and timeline impact of scope changes
- **Product Manager**: Advise on technical trade-offs and long-term maintainability implications
- **QA/Testing**: Define test strategies for complex technical components

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts translate business and stakeholder requirements into clear, actionable technical tasks. They clarify acceptance criteria, document requirements, and support backlog refinement to ensure alignment between business goals and implementation.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Translate business needs into clear user stories and acceptance criteria
- Support backlog refinement and prioritization discussions
- Clarify ambiguities and edge cases with stakeholders and the team
- Validate that implemented solutions meet business requirements
- Document business rules and process flows
- Identify dependencies and integration points with other systems

### Goals
- Ensure requirements are clear and unambiguous before development starts
- Reduce rework and scope creep through thorough requirements gathering
- Bridge communication between business stakeholders and technical teams

### Typical Communication
- Requirement documentation and user story specifications
- Backlog refinement meetings and acceptance criteria clarification
- Stakeholder interviews and feedback sessions
- Status updates on requirement validation

### Collaboration Touchpoints
- **Product Manager**: Align on business prioritization and success metrics
- **Developers**: Clarify technical feasibility and discuss implementation approaches
- **Project Manager**: Support scope management and timeline planning
- **QA/Testing**: Ensure acceptance criteria are testable and comprehensive

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers plan and coordinate production releases. They maintain the release calendar, oversee deployment readiness, and manage communication between engineering, QA, and stakeholders during go-live events to ensure smooth and risk-free deployments.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and schedule production releases
- Maintain a release calendar and communicate timelines to stakeholders
- Oversee pre-release checklists and deployment readiness activities
- Coordinate between engineering, QA, product, and support teams
- Manage release documentation and communications
- Oversee post-deployment verification and monitoring
- Lead incident response and rollback procedures if issues arise
- Maintain release notes and communicate changes to users and support teams

### Goals
- Execute smooth, predictable, and risk-free deployments
- Minimize downtime and customer impact during releases
- Maintain clear communication and alignment across all teams during go-live
- Enable rapid rollback or mitigation if critical issues are detected

### Typical Communication
- Release planning meetings with stakeholders
- Pre-release readiness checklists and sign-offs
- Release notes and user communications
- Post-release retrospectives and incident reports

### Collaboration Touchpoints
- **Project Manager**: Coordinate release timing with project milestones and priorities
- **Developers**: Ensure code is deployment-ready and coordinate hot-fix procedures
- **QA/Testing**: Verify smoke tests are complete and sign off on release readiness
- **Product Manager**: Communicate feature availability and release notes to customers
- **Support/Operations**: Provide release details and prepare support teams for customer inquiries
